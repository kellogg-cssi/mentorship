INTRODUCTION

The “Finding Curie” program was developed with the concept in mind that intellect and creativity are equally distributed, but opportunity is not.

Our premier mentorship program is designed to close that gap by allowing rising scientists in under-developed countries or under represented communities to be mentored by established researchers in developed countries. We believe that mentorship provided by senior researchers at elite institutions in developed countries to junior researchers may prove to be among the most impactful mechanisms for eliminating these barriers and growing research advances worldwide.

OUR GOAL
The goal of our mentorship program is to connect top scholars in countries with leading scientific accomplishments to junior scientists in relatively under-developed countries or communities to enable more scientific discovery, and the diversity of such, to flourish.

The global academic research system is fraught with inequities. Elite institutions in developed countries overwhelmingly dominate the creation of scientific knowledge. Given that success often begets more success, this creates an unintended consequence that those in developed countries disproportionately shape research priorities and methodological standards. In addition to the equity dimension, the accumulation of scientific knowledge is disadvantaged worldwide when talented individuals are excluded from opportunities because of the country or social class into which they were born.

If you're looking forward to a transformational opportunity to change your scientific career and advancements in science itself, please join our program!

Q & A

Who are the mentors and targeted mentees?

Our mentors are leading scholars in elite institutions with high-impact publications in top journals.

Targeted mentees include PhD students/postdocs/junior faculty from developing countries, looking for a transformational opportunity to change your academic career

Should I pay for this mentorship program?

It is totally free.

Can I choose mentor/mentee by myself?                                

Currently, we do not have the resources to accommodate such requests. Our matching system will choose the most suitable mentor/mentee for you in terms of research interest and if, for some reason, the connection is less than ideal, you can request a re-match.

What should I do as a mentee?      

Apply by clicking the sign-up link. Then, submit your drafts as well as your personal information via the EasyChair system. We'll notify you within X weeks regarding next steps.

What benefits will I gain from this mentorship program as a mentee?      

1. Access to a semi-structured mentorship relationship with an established scholar with impactful papers
2. Constructive criticism on your publication drafts
3. Trusted advise from your mentor for further study
4. Membership in our community with access to various resources 

What should I do as a mentor?      

You’ll receive review notification via email, which is the same as other anonymous peer-review process. Please submit your feedback within 2 weeks, and we'll notify you within one week regarding next steps.
