INTRODUCTION

The RISE mentorship program emerged from the principle that intellect and creativity are evenly distributed, but opportunities are not.

RISE is designed to reduce the opportunity gap by inviting rising scholars in developing countries to engage in mentorship sessions with established researchers from highly-ranked research institutions. Mentorship programs offer a powerful set of tools that can help junior scholars to succeed.

Specifically, RISE facilitates mentor-mentee discussions on working papers aimed at helping to prepare junior scholars for the peer-review process. Junior scholars interested in the program are invited to register and upload their working papers. The program systematically matches qualified submissions to suitable senior scholars, i.e., mentors, for review. Then, mentees will receive comments/feedbacks from their mentors, and mentor/mentee pairs will schedule a 30-minute discussion of potential revisions and research directions.

If you would like to discuss your working paper with a senior scholar in the field, please join our program!

Q & A

Who are the mentors and targeted mentees?

Our mentors are leading scholars from highly-ranked institutions.

Targeted mentees include Ph.D. students/postdocs/junior faculty from developing countries, looking for a transformational opportunity to change their academic careers.

Is there a cost for the mentorship program? 

It is totally free.

Can I choose my mentor/mentee by myself?                                

Currently, we do not have the resources to accommodate such requests. Our matching system will choose a suitable mentor/mentee for you in terms of research interest and if, for some reason, the connection is less than ideal, you can request a re-match.

What should I do as a mentee?      

Apply by clicking the sign-up link. Then, submit your drafts as well as your personal information via the EasyChair system. We'll notify you within three weeks regarding the next steps.

What benefits will I gain from this mentorship program as a mentee?      

1. Access to a semi-structured mentorship relationship with an established scholar with impactful papers
2. Constructive criticism on your publication drafts
3. Trusted advice from your mentor on future work

What should I do as a mentor?      

You’ll receive a review notification via email, which is the same as other anonymous peer-review processes. Please submit your feedback within 2 weeks, and we'll notify you within one week regarding the next steps.
